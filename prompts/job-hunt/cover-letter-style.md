# Cover Letter Style Guide

Write a cover letter using this voice and style:

## Tone
- Highly educated but slightly casual
- Confident, energetic, and passionate (NOT bubbly or overly enthusiastic)
- No corporate filler, buzzwords, or try-hard language

## Structure
- Keep it shorter and punchy
- Open with industry insight, problem/solution focus, or direct value statement
- End with confident but humble call to action

## Experience Framing
- Present experience as diverse problem-solving and continuous learning (not seniority or "seen it all")
- Position as technical leader or seasoned individual contributor
- Use general capability statements rather than specific metrics

## Company Connection
- Show industry alignment with enough company knowledge to demonstrate basic research
- Avoid forced enthusiasm about specific company strategies or missions

## Key Themes
- Problem-solving ability and willingness to learn
- Cross-industry and tech stack experience
- Leadership, communication, and teamwork skills

## Phrases to AVOID
- "I'm excited to apply"
- "Your mission to [X] resonates deeply with me"
- "Your tech stack aligns perfectly with my expertise"
- "I thrive in fast-paced environments"
- "Synergistic solutions"
- Any overly corporate or buzzword language

## Closing Example
"I believe my experience would be valuable to your team, and I'd appreciate the chance to explore this opportunity further."
