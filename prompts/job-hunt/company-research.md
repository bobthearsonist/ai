# Company Research Prompt for Senior Software Engineering Role

**Target Company:** [Company Name]

## Phase 1: Company Fundamentals

Research and provide the following information:

### Financial & Business Metrics
- Funding stage and total funding raised
- Latest valuation (if available)
- Revenue (or revenue range/estimates)
- Year founded and growth trajectory
- Recent financial news or funding rounds

### Company Profile
- Company size (total employees, engineering team size)
- Headquarters location and office locations
- Business model and target market
- Key products/services
- Main competitors

### Workplace Insights
- Glassdoor rating and key themes from reviews (specifically engineering reviews)
- Levels.fyi compensation data for senior engineers
- Remote/hybrid/in-office policy
- Tech stack and engineering culture indicators
- Recent news about layoffs, hiring freezes, or expansion

## Phase 2: LinkedIn Network Analysis

### Step 1: Identify Key Engineering Leaders
Find and list 5-10 people in these roles:
- VP/Director of Engineering
- Engineering Managers
- Principal/Staff Engineers
- CTO or technical co-founders

For each person, note:
- Current title and tenure at company
- Previous companies (last 2-3 positions)

### Step 2: Build the Relationship Graph
Analyze the identified leaders' work histories to map:

1. **Internal connections:** Which of these leaders previously worked together at the target company or elsewhere?
2. **Company clusters:** What companies appear multiple times in their backgrounds? (e.g., "3 leaders came from Google, 2 from Stripe")
3. **Career paths:** Common trajectories (e.g., "several leaders came from Big Tech → Series B startup → here")

### Step 3: Second-Degree Analysis
For 2-3 of the most interesting leaders, identify:
- 3-5 senior/staff engineers currently at the company who previously worked with them
- Pattern: Do they tend to bring their teams with them?

### Output Format
Create a visual representation showing:
```
[Company Name] Engineering Leadership Network

Previous Overlaps:
- Leader A + Leader B: Both at Google (2018-2020)
- Leader A + Engineer C: Both at Stripe (2020-2022)
- Leader D + Leader E: Both at [Startup X] (2019-2021)

Company Pipelines:
- Google → 4 people
- Meta → 2 people
- Stripe → 3 people

Insight: This team has strong connections from [pattern you notice]
```

## Phase 3: Assessment Questions

Based on the research, answer:
1. **Stability signals:** Are leaders recent hires or long-tenured? Any recent departures?
2. **Team quality:** Do leaders have track records at respected companies?
3. **Culture indicators:** Is this a "team that works together repeatedly" or more fragmented?
4. **Growth stage:** Does the leadership team match the company's current stage?

## Red Flags to Note
- Frequent leadership turnover
- Recent mass departures from engineering
- Significant funding without matching team growth
- Negative Glassdoor themes around engineering culture
